Exam Overview

TEF Exam

The Test d'Évaluation de Français evaluates French language skills for academic, professional or immigration purposes.

  • Valid for 2 years
  • 5 sections: Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking, Lexicon & Structure
  • 6 levels from A1 to C2

TCF Exam

The Test de Connaissance du Français assesses general French language proficiency.

  • Valid for 2 years
  • 3 compulsory sections: Listening, Reading, Language Structures
  • Optional Speaking and Writing sections
  • 6 levels from A1 to C2

Exam Skills Breakdown

Listening Comprehension

Tests ability to understand spoken French in various contexts (conversations, announcements, interviews).

Marking Scheme:

  • Points awarded for correct answers
  • No penalty for wrong answers
  • Score converted to CEFR level (A1-C2)

Common Mistakes:

  • Misunderstanding key words
  • Focusing too much on unknown words
  • Not paying attention to intonation and context clues

Time Management:

  • Approx. 40 minutes for TEF (60 questions)
  • Approx. 25 minutes for TCF (29 questions)
  • Read questions before listening
  • Use second listening to confirm answers

Sample Question:

You will hear a conversation between two people. Answer the question based on what you hear.

Question: Why is the woman calling the restaurant?

Useful Phrases:

  • "D'après l'enregistrement..." (According to the recording...)
  • "L'interlocuteur mentionne que..." (The speaker mentions that...)
  • "On peut comprendre que..." (We can understand that...)

Reading Comprehension

Evaluates ability to understand written French (articles, advertisements, letters, etc.).

Marking Scheme:

  • Points for correct answers
  • No negative marking
  • Score converted to CEFR level

Common Mistakes:

  • Spending too much time on difficult questions
  • Not reading instructions carefully
  • Answering based on personal knowledge rather than text

Time Management:

  • Approx. 60 minutes for TEF (50 questions)
  • Approx. 45 minutes for TCF (29 questions)
  • Skim text first for general understanding
  • Read questions before detailed reading

Sample Question:

Read the following advertisement:

"Grande vente d'hiver! Tous les manteaux à -50% ce week-end seulement. Profitez-en!"

Question: What is being advertised?

Useful Phrases:

  • "Le texte indique que..." (The text indicates that...)
  • "Il est mentionné que..." (It is mentioned that...)
  • "On peut déduire que..." (We can deduce that...)

Writing Expression

Assesses ability to produce clear, coherent written French (emails, letters, essays).

Marking Criteria:

  • Task completion (30%)
  • Coherence and cohesion (25%)
  • Lexical range and accuracy (25%)
  • Grammatical accuracy (20%)

Common Mistakes:

  • Not addressing all parts of the task
  • Poor paragraph structure
  • Repetitive vocabulary
  • Basic grammar errors

Time Management:

  • 60 minutes for both TEF and TCF
  • Plan (10 min), Write (40 min), Review (10 min)
  • Word count: 150-200 words for TEF, 120-150 for TCF

Sample Task:

You recently stayed at a hotel and were dissatisfied with the service. Write an email to the hotel manager (150-200 words) including:

  • Details of your stay
  • Problems encountered
  • Suggestions for improvement

Write your response here...

Useful Phrases:

  • "Je me permets de vous écrire concernant..." (I am writing to you regarding...)
  • "Tout d'abord,... Ensuite,... Enfin,..." (First,... Then,... Finally,...)
  • "Je vous serais reconnaissant de..." (I would be grateful if you...)

Speaking Expression

Evaluates oral communication skills through interviews, role-plays and presentations.

Marking Criteria:

  • Fluency and coherence (30%)
  • Lexical resource (25%)
  • Grammatical range and accuracy (25%)
  • Pronunciation (20%)

Common Mistakes:

  • Long pauses and hesitation
  • Not answering the question asked
  • Monotonous intonation
  • Overusing simple structures

Time Management:

  • 15 minutes for TEF (3 tasks)
  • 12 minutes for TCF (3 tasks)
  • Balance preparation and speaking time
  • Keep answers concise but complete

Sample Task:

You are applying for a job in a French company. The interviewer asks:

"Pourquoi voulez-vous travailler dans notre entreprise?" (Why do you want to work for our company?)

Prepare your response...

Useful Phrases:

  • "À mon avis,..." (In my opinion...)
  • "Je dirais que..." (I would say that...)
  • "D'un côté,... De l'autre côté,..." (On one hand,... On the other hand...)

Preparation Strategies

For Teachers

Classroom Activities:

  • Mock exams under timed conditions
  • Peer evaluation of writing and speaking
  • Error correction sessions
  • Listening comprehension with authentic materials

Teaching Strategies:

  • Focus on exam format and question types
  • Teach time management techniques
  • Provide regular feedback on progress
  • Create vocabulary lists by theme

Recommended Exercises:

  • Weekly writing assignments with model answers
  • Daily listening practice with transcripts
  • Structured speaking practice in pairs
  • Grammar drills targeting common errors

For Students

Self-Study Plan:

  • Daily vocabulary building (15-20 words)
  • Regular listening practice (podcasts, news)
  • Writing practice with timed exercises
  • Speaking practice with language partners

Stress Management:

  • Practice relaxation techniques (deep breathing)
  • Simulate exam conditions at home
  • Maintain healthy sleep schedule
  • Positive self-talk and visualization

Exam Day Tips:

  • Arrive early and prepared
  • Read instructions carefully
  • Manage time effectively
  • Stay calm and focused

Grammar Expectations by Level

Level Expected Grammar
A1-A2 Present tense, basic questions, simple negation, articles, common prepositions
B1 Past tenses (passé composé, imparfait), future, conditional, pronouns, comparatives
B2 Subjunctive, complex sentence structures, passive voice, reported speech
C1-C2 Advanced syntax, nuanced tenses, stylistic variation, idiomatic expressions
